Anderson wins gold in Lady's Snowboard Slopestyle Finals February 9, 2014

Jamie Anderson gave the United States a gold-medal sweep in the first Olympic slopestyle snowboarding competition on Sunday, while Jenny Jones took bronze to win Britain’s first medal in any snow sport. Anderson soared to victory in a near flawless run in the women’s final, a day after Sage Kotsenburg captured the men’s gold medal. The 23-year-old from South Lake Tahoe, California, did it, as is her fashion, when the moment absolutely required it.

Anderson had expressed concern about the difficulty of the course during training, particularly after a jarring crash in practice on Wednesday left her with an aching back. She watched as competitor after competitor struggled to navigate the series of rails and jumps that the Finnish snowboarder Peetu Piiroinen feared was too big for women. At times, it looked like it.
